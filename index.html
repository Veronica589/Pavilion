    Frequently Asked Questions

    The list below contains a list of most commonly asked questions based on a survey conducted in 2021 by Google.
    We hope that by going through this section many a good number of your questions will be answered.

    • Does Pavilion accept NHIF cards as a mode of payment?

      Yes we do!In the year 2018 Pavilion joined the list of hospitals that have a partnership with the National Health Insurance Fund.
      Both inpatients and outpatients have the priviledge to have the burden of some of their hospital bill taken care by the government.

    • What capacity does Pavilion have as a Covid-19 Infection control centre?

      Pavilion is well capable of handling Covid-19 patients.
      The hospital has an overall bed capacity of 650 beds, 24 of which are ICU beds and 10 HDU beds which are well equipped.

    • How well is Pavilion enabled as a cancer treatment centre?

      From chemotherapy to radiology Pavilion has the capacity to handle cancer patients. The hospital has especially dedicated an Intergrated Molecular Imaging Centre (IMIC) for cancer screening and treatment.
      We especially hope that this will reduce the number of Kenyans that fly out of the country to seek medical attention.

    • Does Pavilion offer Covid-19 vaccination?

      Yes Pavilion does offer Covid-19 vaccination. We hope to help the ministry of health to reach it's target of vaccinated all Kenyan adults(18yrs and above) by June 2022.
      We hope to this by offering the vaccination in three phases as earlier suggested.

    • How does Pavilion hope to contribute in the fight against early childhood pregnancies?

      Pavilion has joined hands with the inua dada initiative to help prevent early pregnancies especially in marginalised areas.
      This is to be done by educating both the girl-child and boy-child on the consequences of this great aftermath. We also hope to educate communities to do away with these archaic traditions.

    • Since Pavilion is an upcoming hospital, how does it hope to battle corruption especially in the health care sector?

      Well, intergrity and work ethic are our core principles here at Pavilion.We hope to keep up our A-game in centuries to come.
      We will provide quality healthcare to anyone in need who comes our way regardless of their background, tribe or race.
